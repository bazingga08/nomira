# Naming Skill — Architecture Spec (Company-Replacement Mandate)

*Spec authored 2026-05-31. Goal: not "an expert" but a skill/agent that replaces an entire brand-naming FIRM and the full education behind it. The skill must be a self-driving agentic pipeline — it interrogates, gathers, debates, decides, and only then outputs. No thin one-line outputs.*

---

## The mandate (Sardhak's words, distilled)
1. **Absorb the whole curriculum.** Train on everything a person earning a degree in this field studies — the books, articles, courses, exam questions, the academic cycles they go through. Understand what they learned and how they think.
2. **Replicate the whole firm, not one role.** A naming firm is an org: strategy, creative invention, linguistics, a global linguist network, trademark/legal, domain/digital, consumer research, cultural evaluation, decision/synthesis. Encode each as a role the skill performs.
3. **The skill owns its own process.** It must, by itself: interrogate the brief (ask all the right questions), gather every input it needs, run multi-agent debate to resolve debatable calls, reference how similar companies operate, and self-drive through every stage to a complete output package — without the user having to spell out the steps.
4. **Deep research before acting.** Study competing firms and approaches as reference. Do the due diligence the skill would expect of itself.

## Roles to encode (the "company" inside the skill)
- **Brand Strategist** — intake, Diamond Framework, positioning, behavior/experience definition, naming territories.
- **Creative Namer team(s)** — divergent generation; multiple small teams; some on deliberate misdirection briefs; separate creation from judgment.
- **Phonosemantics Linguist** — sound symbolism, morphology, pronounceability, rhythm.
- **Global/Cross-cultural Linguist network** — connotation & disaster checks across target languages.
- **Trademark/IP Counsel** — distinctiveness spectrum (generic→descriptive→suggestive→arbitrary→fanciful), Nice classes, conflict/clearance reasoning.
- **Domain/Digital Strategist** — URL/handle availability and strategy (don't let domain veto a superior name).
- **Consumer Researcher** — competitor-test framing, message delivery / believability (not "do you like it").
- **Semiotician** — meaning, symbolism, cultural codes.
- **Decision/Synthesis Lead** — scoring, resolving internal debate, final recommendation with rationale + risks.

## Curriculum / body of knowledge to absorb (the "degree")
- **Onomastics** (the academic study of names)
- **Phonetics & phonology; psycholinguistics; phonosemantics / sound symbolism**
- **Morphology & word formation**
- **Semiotics & semantics**
- **Branding & marketing theory** (brand equity, positioning; Aaker; Keller's CBBE)
- **Consumer psychology & behavioral science** (processing fluency, memory, the "lazy brain")
- **Trademark / IP law fundamentals** (distinctiveness spectrum, Nice classification, likelihood of confusion)
- **Cross-cultural linguistics & localization**
- **Cognitive science of memory & recall**

## The pipeline the SKILL runs at invocation (self-driving)
- **A — Intake / Interrogation:** never output before gathering. Run a structured question battery (Diamond Framework + audience, category, competitors, tone/personality, target markets & languages, legal/trademark scope, longevity & extensibility, hard constraints). If the user doesn't answer, state explicit assumptions and proceed.
- **B — Strategy synthesis:** positioning brief + naming territories/themes.
- **C — Divergent generation:** many candidates across all name types & techniques, including misdirection; separate creation from judgment.
- **D — Internal debate:** role-agents argue from their lenses (linguist vs. counsel vs. strategist vs. consumer) to stress-test survivors.
- **E — Screening gauntlet:** linguistic disaster check, trademark distinctiveness, domain, cultural connotation.
- **F — Scoring & shortlist:** rubric with weights; rationale per name; competitor-test framing; risk flags.
- **G — Output package:** shortlist + rationale + screening notes + recommended next steps (full clearance, consumer test).

## Build sequencing
- **Workflow 1 (running):** foundation research → adversarial verify → hardened KB v2 → v1 skill → validation + gap list.
- **Workflow 2 (queued, launches when WF1 lands):** consumes WF1's KB v2 + gap list; absorbs the academic curriculum; maps firm org → role-personas; runs an architect debate panel to design the self-driving pipeline; rebuilds the skill as the agentic, multi-role pipeline above; validates with adversarial judges + a gap loop until Lexicon-grade.
